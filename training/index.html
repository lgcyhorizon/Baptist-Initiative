Training & Education

Equipping the local church to counsel.

From foundational training in your home church to regional conferences and one-day symposiums — here is how we equip you.

Home · Training
Five Doors In

Find the path that fits your church & calling.

All five training pathways draw on the same uniform curriculum that anchors our certification process.

TC

Training Centers

Approved Training Centers deliver our uniform certification curriculum and may supervise applicants for counseling hours. They also offer additional training and education at their discretion.

RM

Regional Mentors

Approved mentors not connected with a training center serve their region by teaching the curriculum and supervising counseling hours.

CF

Conferences

Multi-day national and regional conferences feature deep-dive teaching, case studies, and gathering with the broader biblical counseling family.

SY

One-day Symposiums

Focused training on a single topic — anger, anxiety, marriage, parenting — ideal for pastors, small-group leaders, and laypeople.

CH

Training in Your Church

We’ll bring the Foundations course to your church. A trained instructor walks your congregation through the eleven-session Phase One curriculum.

The Three Levels

How the training is structured.

Whether you take the training as part of your certification path or as standalone equipping, the same three-level structure applies.

01

Level One — Foundations of Biblical Counseling

Eleven two-hour sessions covering Introduction, Helping People Change, Man’s Way vs. God’s Way, Biblical Change (Parts One & Two), Dealing with Self, Interpersonal Problems, Marriage, Parenting, Depression & Anxiety, and Besetting Sin.

02

Level Two — Process of Biblical Counseling

Four two-hour sessions: Involvement & Inspiration; Inventory & Interpretation; Instruction & Inducement; Implementation & Integration.

03

Level Three — Theology of Biblical Counseling

Four two-hour sessions on Bibliology, Theology Proper (Doctrine of God), Anthropology, Christology, Soteriology, Pneumatology, and Ecclesiology.
